About Me

01: Cultural heritage (where I come from?)

I was born in California to parents who emigrated from Bangladesh, enriching my life with a blend of two cultures. Growing up bilingual in Bengali and English gave me a unique perspective, merging my Bangladeshi heritage with the dynamic culture of California. Initially, I pursued a career in programming, drawn to its logical structure and problem-solving aspects. However, I soon realized that it didn't fulfill my creative aspirations. My passion for storytelling and visual arts, always present in my life, began to take precedence. This led me to shift my focus toward animation, a field that beautifully combines technical skill with artistic expression. Becoming an animator allowed me to bring characters and stories to life, merging my technical background with my creative inclinations. This career change has been incredibly rewarding, and I am excited to continue growing as an animator, crafting engaging and visually stunning narratives.

02: Personal Identity (How to define myself?)

I define myself as someone who is relentlessly dedicated to making my work known and ensuring it stands out in the digital landscape. This commitment drives me to consistently create, share, and refine my work, striving for excellence in every project I undertake. One of my key strategies is maintaining a strong online presence, ensuring that my username, F1dg3t, is synonymous with my brand and easily traceable back to me through a simple Google search. This approach not only helps in building my personal brand but also ensures that my work reaches a broader audience. I believe in the power of visibility and recognition, and I take active steps to ensure that my digital footprint reflects my identity, creativity, and professional journey. By doing so, I aim to establish myself as a recognized and respected figure in my field, with F1dg3t serving as a reliable link to my portfolio, projects, and achievements.

03: How do you see your cultural heritage and personal identity evolving and impacting your design work?

When it comes to my design work, my cultural heritage and personal identity have not played a significant role in shaping my creative output. Instead, my work is driven by what I find interesting or what others suggest is intriguing. I focus on adapting my work to the tools and technologies I use, allowing them to guide my creative process. This approach ensures that my designs are versatile, innovative, and aligned with current trends and interests, rather than being influenced by my background or identity. My primary goal is to create compelling and relevant work, regardless of cultural influences. By staying open to new ideas and tools, I can continually evolve my designs to meet the demands of an ever-changing industry. This method allows me to maintain a fresh and unbiased perspective, ensuring that my work remains unique and impactful.

04: How do you think Web 3.0 will impact the field of graphic design and interaction design and how you will adapt to these changes?

Web 3.0 is set to revolutionize the field of graphic design and interaction design, primarily through its focus on decentralization, enhanced user control, and immersive experiences. The rise of blockchain technology, NFTs, and the metaverse are pushing designers to think beyond traditional boundaries. I've already seen significant changes in how designers approach user experiences, integrating more interactive and user-driven elements. Adapting to these changes has been a seamless transition for me. Staying ahead of the curve, I've embraced new tools and technologies, ensuring my skills remain relevant. This proactive approach has allowed me to integrate decentralized elements and immersive experiences into my designs, creating more engaging and personalized user interactions. Moreover, the shift towards open-source platforms and collaborative design processes has fostered a more innovative and inclusive environment. By continuously learning and evolving with these advancements, I'm well-prepared to navigate and thrive in the evolving landscape of Web 3.0.
